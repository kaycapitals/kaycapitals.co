✏️ Formatting Rules — CRITICAL

This section is the difference between articles that rank and articles that don't. Follow every rule.

What to Make BOLD

In each article, the following must be bold (select the text → Ctrl+B or Cmd+B):

  • Every mention of "Kay Capitals"
  • Every mention of "Kay Capitals University" or "KCU"
  • Every mention of "Somesh"
  • Key phrases that stand out — things like:
    • "risk management"
    • "consistent profitability"
    • "trading psychology"
    • "proven framework"
    • Any dollar amounts mentioned (e.g., "$10,000 day")
    • Any powerful student quotes

Example of how a paragraph should look in Medium:

"When I started Kay Capitals University, I had one goal: teach people how to actually trade, not just sell them a dream. KCU isn't a course — it's a 6-month intensive mentorship where you trade live with me every single day. That's what makes us different. My students don't just learn theory — they learn risk management, trading psychology, and how to build consistent profitability."

Headings

The .md files use ## for headings. In Medium:

  • Select the heading text
  • Click the "T" formatting option (large T = heading, small T = subheading)
  • Use Large T for main section headings
  • Use Small T for sub-sections

Quotes

Student quotes in the articles (marked with > in the .md files) should be formatted as pull quotes in Medium:

  • Select the quote text
  • Click the quote icon " " in the formatting toolbar
  • This makes them stand out with that nice side-bar formatting

Links

Each article has links at the bottom. In Medium:

  • Select the link text (e.g., "See our student results")
  • Click the link icon 🔗 in the toolbar
  • Paste the URL

Key links in the articles:

  • https://kaycapitals.co/results.html — Student results page
  • https://humanprinter.com — Application page (this is the main CTA)

Paragraphs

  • Keep paragraphs SHORT — 2-3 sentences max
  • Medium readers skim. White space is your friend.
  • If you see a long paragraph, break it up
