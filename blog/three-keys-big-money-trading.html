Three Keys to Making Big Money Day Trading (From a Full-Time Trader)

By Somesh | Kay Capitals University

You made your plan. Everything looks perfect. It's go time. And then... you don't execute. Something in your head stops you. The trade runs without you, and you sit there watching money you should have made disappear into the chart.

Or worse — you get married to a setup that's clearly not working, refuse to adapt, and lose big.

Sound familiar? I've been there. I'm Somesh, a full-time day trader and founder of Kay Capitals University. Let me tell you the three things that finally unlocked consistent income for me in day trading.

Key #1: Know Your Numbers Before You Enter

Before you click buy or sell, you need to know three things:

  1. Position size — How much capital are you putting in?
  2. Stop loss — Where are you getting out if it goes wrong?
  3. Profit target — Where are you taking money off the table?

If you don't know any one of these, you're not ready to trade. Period.

Here's why this matters: If you don't know what you stand to lose, you'll lose too much. If you don't know what you stand to gain, you won't know how much to risk.

Let's say you're trading SPY options. Five contracts. Risking 30 cents per contract. Worst case: you lose $150. Best case: you make $100 per contract, or $500 total.

Now the math is clear: you're risking $150 to make $500. That's a 3:1 reward-to-risk ratio. When you see the trade in those terms, execution becomes easier because you understand both sides of the equation.

This is the foundation of how to make money day trading. Not hoping. Not guessing. Knowing your numbers.

Key #2: Be Flexible — Don't Marry Your Setup

This one cost me a lot of money early on.

I remember a morning in early 2020. I did everything right — woke up early, followed my routine, built my trading plan by 8:30 AM. Then economic data came out, and the market shifted. Everything pointed bearish. But my plan said long.

Instead of adapting, I stuck with my original plan. I got married to my setup. And I lost so much money I still don't like talking about it.

The market doesn't care about your plan. Your plan is a starting point, not a contract. When conditions change, you change with them. The best traders in the world are flexible. They read the room, adjust, and execute based on what's actually happening — not what they hoped would happen.

Here's the balance: Be very careful when you're planning. Be very bold when you're executing.

In the pre-market, be picky. Scrutinize everything. But once the bell rings and your setup is confirmed, don't hesitate. Execute with conviction.

Key #3: If You Know Your Stop Loss, Nothing Can Hurt You

This is the most powerful mindset shift in day trading, and it came from a conversation with a trader friend of mine.

I had made a perfect plan. Didn't execute. The market went exactly where I said it would. I missed the entire move because I was afraid. I told my friend, and his response was one sentence:

"What if it worked?"

Then he said something that changed my trading forever:

"If you know where your stop loss is, there's literally nothing in the stock market that can hurt you."

Think about that. If your max loss is defined before you enter — if you KNOW the absolute worst-case scenario — what are you afraid of? You've already accepted the risk. There's nothing left to fear.

The next day, I applied this. Made my plan. Knew my stop loss. Executed. Made $500 just by following the plan I already had. No special moves. No complicated strategy. Just execution with defined risk.

Day trading for income isn't about being right every time. It's about having a system where your wins outweigh your losses over time, and having the courage to execute because you've already accepted the worst-case outcome.

The Difference Between Having a Stop Loss and Respecting It

I have to call this out because it's a trap. Knowing your stop loss and actually getting out when it hits are two completely different skills.

When the trade goes against you and price is approaching your stop, your brain will negotiate. "Maybe it'll come back." "Let me give it more room." "It looks okay on the 15-minute chart."

No. When it hits, you're out. If the trade reverses after you exit, you can re-enter. But sitting in a losing position hoping for a miracle is how accounts get blown.

This is something we hammer home inside KCU. Having the stop loss is step one. Accepting it is where the real growth happens.

Action Steps: Start Making Money Day Trading

Here's what to do after reading this:

  1. Define your position size before every trade — know how much you're risking in dollars
  2. Set your stop loss based on chart structure, not how much you're willing to lose emotionally
  3. Know your profit target so you can calculate reward-to-risk before entry
  4. Stay flexible — adjust your plan when the market tells you to
  5. Execute with conviction — once the setup confirms, trust your preparation and pull the trigger

The formula is simple. The execution is hard. That's why most people lose money day trading — not because the market is impossible, but because they can't get out of their own way.
