How to Handle Trading Losses: Brutally Honest Advice From a Trader With 8+ Years of Scars

By Somesh | Kay Capitals University

Here's something nobody in the trading world wants to tell you: not all losses are mistakes.

Read that again. Let it sink in.

I'm Somesh, I've been day trading for over eight years. I run Kay Capitals University where I trade live every day with students who are serious about this game. And after all those years, here's the truth: I still take losses. Every single trader does.

The difference between the traders who make it and the ones who blow up isn't about avoiding losses. It's about knowing how to handle trading losses the right way.

The Shift That Changes Everything

Most traders treat every loss like a personal failure. They beat themselves up, question their strategy, and carry that negative energy into the next trade — which then becomes another loss.

Here's the cliché that will change your trading: stop looking at losses as failures. Start looking at them as data.

The Two Types of Trading Losses

This is the concept most traders never grasp, and it's why they stay stuck for years.

Type 1: Losses From Your Mistakes

These are losses where you did something wrong. You entered too late. You didn't respect your stop-loss. You oversized because you were emotional. You ignored your plan.

These losses are gold. Not because they feel good, but because they give you something to fix. There's a lesson embedded in the pain. Extract it.

Type 2: Losses From the Natural Distribution

These are losses where you did everything right and the trade still didn't work.

If your strategy wins 80% of the time, that means 20 out of every 100 trades will be losers. Not because you screwed up. Just because that's how probability works.

Here's where it gets dangerous: most traders try to learn from these losses. They dig into trades that were executed perfectly, looking for something that went wrong. Because they can't find anything real, they start "fixing" things that were never broken. Now they've introduced a new variable into a working system.

Which Losses Deserve Your Energy?

  • Mistake losses: Study them. Journal them. Fix them.
  • Statistical losses: Acknowledge them. Move on. Don't touch anything.

When Losses Become Truly Dangerous

The danger isn't the loss itself. It's the chain reaction:

Loss → Frustration → Anxiety → Doubt → Bigger mistakes

You take a loss. You get frustrated. The frustration creates anxiety: Is my system broken? The anxiety creates doubt: Am I cut out for this? The doubt destroys your execution on the next trade.

The Power of Journaling Your Trades

You think you'll remember what you did six months ago? You won't. But if you journal, you can go back and find patterns you'd never see otherwise.

Here's a real example: I noticed I was losing money every single Tuesday. Profitable Monday. Profitable Wednesday through Friday. But Tuesday was bleeding me dry, week after week.

I only found this because I was journaling. So the next Tuesday, I went small. First trade — small loss, no big deal. Second trade set up beautifully, and because I was still in the game with a calm mindset, I took it and recovered everything.

Over time, your journal will reveal:

  • Your best setups and your worst setups
  • What time of day you perform best
  • What leads to your biggest mistakes
  • Which stocks you trade well and which destroy you

Think Monthly, Not Daily

Out of roughly 20 trading days in a month, you're going to lose on 5-6 of them. That's normal. That's expected.

If you can control your losses on those days — keep them small, don't spiral — you make your money on the remaining 14-15 winning days. Zoom out. Think bigger. One bad day doesn't define your month.

Learn From Others' Mistakes Too

You don't have to learn everything from your own losses. Join a community. Read other traders' journals. Watch breakdowns. Absorb their lessons so you can skip their pain.

The Bottom Line

There are losses you can control and losses you can't. Stress over the ones you can't, and you'll destroy yourself for no reason. Fix the ones you can, and you'll grow exponentially.
