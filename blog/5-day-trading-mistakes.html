5 Day Trading Mistakes That Are Killing Your Account (And How to Fix Them)

By Somesh | Kay Capitals University

You take two steps forward, then fall three steps back. Sound familiar? If you keep ending up at square one no matter how hard you try, it's probably because you're making one — or all — of these five common day trading mistakes.

I'm Somesh. I've been day trading full-time for years. I've made every one of these mistakes myself. The difference is I stopped making them — and that's when I started making real money.

Mistake #1: Not Having a Trading Plan

This is the big one. The day trading mistake to avoid above all others. A trading plan isn't just "I think Tesla is going down today." A real trading plan includes:

  • Entry point — Where exactly are you getting in?
  • Reasoning — Why this trade? What's the setup?
  • Stop loss — Where are you getting out if it goes against you?
  • Profit target — Where are you taking money off the table?
  • Risk management — How much are you willing to lose?

Without a plan, you're just gambling. At Kay Capitals, I teach a framework called LTP — Levels, Trends, Patient Candles. That's my entire system:

  • L — Levels: I identify critical support and resistance levels on the 60-minute chart
  • T — Trends: I confirm the direction using trend analysis
  • P — Patient Candles: I wait for a specific candle pattern that meets strict criteria before entering

Mistake #2: Not Respecting Your Stop Loss

Having a stop loss and actually respecting it are two completely different things. Most traders know where their stop loss should be. They just can't pull the trigger when price gets there.

Here's what happens: Price hits your stop, and your brain goes into negotiation mode. "Maybe it'll turn around." "Let me wait for the candle to close." "It looks good on the 15-minute chart."

My rule: The moment price touches my stop loss, I'm out. Period. Not waiting for a candle close. Not switching timeframes. Not hoping.

Mistake #3: Not Doing What the Top 5% Do

You've heard the stat: 95% of traders lose money. The top 5% have a few things in common:

  • They have a proven strategy they follow every day
  • They have criteria for entering trades — not gut feelings
  • They know their entry, stop loss, and profit target before the market opens
  • They have a pre-market routine
  • They take care of themselves — sleep, exercise, discipline

Mistake #4: Trying to Flip Money Instead of Leveraging It

Here's the scenario: Two traders, both with $5,000 accounts.

Trader A has no strategy. They take tiny positions — $200-$300. But since the position is small, they need 100% returns to make any real money. So they're constantly hunting for home runs.

Trader B has a strategy. They take $1,000 positions. They only need 20-30% returns to make the same profit.

Which is easier — catching a 20% move or a 100% move? Stop trying to flip. Start trying to leverage.

Mistake #5: Not Being Mindful of Your Responses

A trade goes against you. What's your response?

Option A: "This market is rigged. I need to make my money back NOW." You revenge trade. You oversize. You lose more.

Option B: "The trade didn't work. I followed my plan. My entry was good, my stop was good — it just didn't go my direction. On to the next one."

Same situation. Completely different outcomes. You always have a choice in how you respond. In trading, that choice is the difference between a bad trade and a blown account.

Quick Recap

  1. No trading plan → Build one with entry, stop, target, and reasoning
  2. Not respecting stop losses → Exit the moment it's hit, no negotiation
  3. Not acting like the top 5% → Build systems, routines, and discipline
  4. Flipping instead of leveraging → Size up with strategy, take consistent profits
  5. Reacting emotionally → Respond to losses with calm and logic
