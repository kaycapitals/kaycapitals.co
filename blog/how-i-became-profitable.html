I Became a Profitable Trader After Doing This One Thing — And It Wasn't a New Strategy

By Somesh | Kay Capitals University

Let me tell you something that's going to sting: I knew how to analyze charts. I knew how to spot trends. I knew how to draw levels and identify the right setups. And I still lost money every single month for 14 months straight.

I'm Somesh — full-time day trader, founder of Kay Capitals University, and someone who's made millions from the market. But before any of that happened, I was crying on my bedroom floor after a brutal trading day, asking myself what the hell I was doing wrong.

The 14 Months of Hell

For my first 14 months of day trading, I was red every single month. Every. Single. One.

I was doing everything "by the book." I was reading charts, watching price action, studying setups. But something was fundamentally off. My trades would eventually go in the direction I predicted — but I was always a step behind. I'd get in too early, get stopped out, watch it rip, and then chase it at the worst possible entry.

The Day Everything Changed

After a particularly rough trading session, I sat down and started crying. Full-on, eyes-out, ugly crying. I was watching everyone else make money while I hemorrhaged my account. And I asked myself one question: What am I doing wrong?

That question forced me to sit down and write something that changed my entire career:

"It's not the market. It's my approach towards the market."

I was too stressed. The fear of losing was clouding my judgment and my decisions. Every trade was emotionally charged. I wasn't trading a system — I was trading my feelings.

The Mindset Shift: Stop Trying to Win Every Trade

From that point on, I shifted my focus from trying to win every single trade to trading the right way. Those sound the same, but they're fundamentally different.

When you're focused on winning, you chase entries. You skip your rules. You FOMO into the middle of a move because you can't stand watching it go without you.

When you're focused on trading the right way, you wait. You follow the process. You let the setup come to you.

The Pullback That Changed Everything

I was watching a stock make a massive move — from $464 up to $467. Old me? I would have jumped in the middle. But I had one simple rule: wait for a pullback.

I trade on a 5-minute chart. My rule was: wait for one red candle. Just one 5-minute red candle, and then I'd get in on the break of its high with my stop loss just below it.

So I watched. The stock kept ripping. Green candle after green candle. No pullback. I didn't touch it. Then it happened. One red candle. The high broke. I went long. Stop loss: 40 cents of risk. The stock ran all the way to $470.

Understanding Waves: The Simple Concept That Makes Trading Click

Stocks move in waves:

  • Wave 1: The initial strong move
  • Wave 2: The pullback (this is where you enter)
  • Wave 3: The continuation move in the original direction

Before I understood this, I was consistently buying at the top of wave one. The pullback would stop me out. Then the stock would rip on wave three without me. Once I started waiting for wave two, everything shifted. My entries improved. My stop losses got tighter. My risk-to-reward went through the roof.

The Fear and Greed Balance

You've heard people say "don't be fearful, don't be greedy." I disagree. You need both.

A little bit of fear keeps you from blowing up. It makes you respect your stop loss, manage your risk, and not bet the farm on a single trade.

A little bit of greed keeps you from scalping pennies. It lets you hold that runner, take half off at your first target, and let the rest ride for a bigger move.

The One Question That Saved My Trading Career

Before every single trade, I started asking myself one question:

"Am I being rational, or emotional?"

Five seconds of self-reflection before clicking the buy button. If the answer was "no, this is just FOMO" — I'd close the chart and walk away. If the answer was "yes, this fits my system" — I'd take the trade.

The Not-To-Do List

Most people make to-do lists. I made a not-to-do list for my trading:

  • Don't chase moves without a pullback
  • Don't trade if you didn't sleep enough
  • Don't trade with a hangover
  • Don't trade if you're emotionally off
  • Don't enter without knowing your stop loss
  • Don't risk more than 1:1 unless the setup is textbook

This flipped everything. When you remove the bad habits, the good trading happens naturally.

It's Strategy PLUS Psychology

I shifted from searching for the perfect strategy to combining strategy with psychology and emotional discipline. That combination has made me millions.

In trading, sometimes the best thing you can do is literally not take a trade. The money you save by sitting out bad setups is just as real as the money you make on good ones.

Consistency Over Home Runs

I don't want you to make $4,000 in a day and lose it all over the next three days. I'd rather you make $400 every single day, consistently, for a year. That's $400 × 252 trading days = $100,800 a year.

That's how to become a profitable trader. Steady growth. Consistency. Not lottery tickets.
