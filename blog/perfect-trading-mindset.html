The Perfect Mindset for Day Trading: Stop Comparing, Start Growing

By Somesh | Kay Capitals University

I need to show you something. This is from my bank account from July 24th, 2018: Minus $152.

That was my entire net worth. Negative. I came from nothing. I'm from a small town in India. I lived off $2 for three days at a time — buying discounted bread that bakeries were about to throw away. And I was comparing myself to traders who were born into money.

I'm Somesh, and today I'm going to talk about the mindset for day trading that took me from that negative bank balance to running Kay Capitals University and trading full time.

Your Relationship With Money Is Everything

Person A: Single parent. $50 is grocery money. Diapers and formula. Every dollar is weighed against survival.

Person B: Born into wealth. $50 is a casual tip.

Now give both of them a $5,000 trading account. Same strategy. Same charts. Same market conditions. Do you think they'll trade the same way? Not a chance.

Your relationship with money — shaped by your upbringing, your experiences, your current situation — directly impacts every trade you take.

The Comparison Trap

I used to compare myself to people who started trading at the same time as me. They were making $1,000 a day while I was still struggling. Even when I was making $100,000 a month, I'd look at someone making a million a month and get depressed.

Here's what I finally realized: the comparison was completely unfair. I came from nothing. The people I was comparing myself to came from money. They had a fundamentally different relationship with risk.

The Account Resistance Story

For months, every time my account approached $100K, I'd self-sabotage. I'd look at the number — $99,991 — and something in my brain would shift. Instead of trading the chart, I was trading the account balance.

Finally, I called a trader I respected. He said: "Do you have any money in your savings? Put $3,000 in your trading account."

I did it. Account went from $98K to $101K overnight. And just like that — the itch was gone. The mental block disappeared.

If you're stuck at a level — $25K, $50K, whatever — consider adding a small deposit to push past it. You're not cheating. You're removing a psychological obstacle.

Managing Stress Scales With Size

If you can't manage the stress of a $1,000 account, a $100,000 account won't fix your problems. It'll make them 100x worse. The first skill you need isn't chart reading. It's stress management.

Practical Mindset for Day Trading

Divide Your Capital

Split your buying power: half for the morning session, half reserved for afternoon opportunities.

Protect Your Gains

If you're up $400 in the morning and decide to trade the afternoon, set a rule: the moment your gains drop to 75% of peak, you're done. No negotiation.

Reassess at Noon

Every day around 12 PM Eastern, step back. Mark new levels. Make a new plan. The market evolves throughout the day.

Don't Have a Directional Bias

If the market goes up, make money. If it goes down, make money. The moment you decide "I think the market will drop today," you've introduced bias. And bias is the enemy.

The Real Edge

After eight years, here's what I know: The edge isn't in the indicators. It isn't in the strategy. It's in you. Your mindset. Your discipline. Your relationship with money. Your ability to manage stress.
