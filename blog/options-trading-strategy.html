The 3-Step A+ Options Trading Strategy That Changed Everything (Full Breakdown)

By Somesh | Kay Capitals University

I've been trading for about eight years. In that time, I've backtested over 30,000 trades, tested dozens of indicators and time frames, and tried just about every strategy under the sun. And after all of that, the best options trading strategy I've ever found can be explained in three steps.

I call it the A+ strategy. And in this post, I'm going to break the entire system down so you can see exactly why it works — and how you can start using it. For a simplified version of this approach, check out our 3-step options strategy.

This isn't flashy. It's not exciting. But this simple options strategy that works has produced days that most people wouldn't believe. And the beauty of it? You only need to trade the first 90 minutes of the day. For real-world results, see how this framework generated $500K in one month.

The 90-Minute Rule That Changed My Life

Before we get into the three steps, let me drop the trading rule that transformed everything for me: you never need more than 90 minutes to day trade.

The first 90 minutes after market open gives you 80% of the cleanest moves for the entire day. After that, the market gets slow, gets choppy, and that's where most traders hand back every dollar they made.

Trade the open. Execute your setups. Walk away. Less stress, less noise, more consistency.

Step 1: Identify Your Key Levels

This is where 99% of traders mess up. They're either drawing random lines from six months ago or they've got 15 indicators stacked on their chart and can't see anything.

Here's exactly what you do: Go to a 60-minute chart. Turn on extended trading hours in your chart settings so you can see pre-market and after-hours data. Switch to a line chart.

Now, draw levels at every bend you see on the line. Every curve, every turn — mark it. Three or four levels above current price, three or four below. Switch back to candlesticks and fine-tune.

These hourly levels are the backbone of the entire options trading strategy. They repeat every single day, and they're the reason I can take clean trades with zero confusion. For more detail on identifying and using these levels, read our complete support and resistance guide.

Why hourly levels? Because smart money uses hourly charts to read supply and demand. That's where the big money reacts.

Mark Your Pre-Market Highs and Lows

Before the bell rings, mark your pre-market high and pre-market low. These two levels instantly tell you whether the market is going to trend or chop.

If prices are stuck between the pre-market high and low — or between yesterday's high and low — you're in the No Trading Zone (NTZ). You do not trade inside that box. Period.

How to Actually Use Levels

Here's the mindset shift that separates amateurs from pros: most traders use levels to get INTO trades. I use levels to get OUT of trades.

That means my levels become profit-taking zones. When price hits a level, I'm scaling out, trimming, letting runners react. I'm not randomly guessing exits — the market already told me where it cares.

Step 2: Confirm the Trend Direction

Levels alone aren't enough. You need to know whether you're trading with the trend or against it. That's where time frames and the 200 Simple Moving Average come in.

Time Frame Structure

Most traders lose because they're on the wrong time frame at the wrong time. Here's my structure:

  • First 30 minutes: 2-minute chart (fast moves, opening range breakout)
  • 10:00 AM – 11:00 AM: 5-minute chart (market slows down, structure becomes clear)
  • After 11:00 AM: 10-minute chart (clean momentum, less noise)

This one change — adjusting time frames throughout the day — removes 80% of fake setups.

The 200 SMA: Your Truth Filter

Add a 200 Simple Moving Average to your chart. Here's the rule:

  • Price above 200 SMA → Bullish bias (think calls)
  • Price below 200 SMA → Bearish bias (think puts)
  • Price chopping around it → Stay out

Opening Range Breakout (ORB)

The Opening Range Breakout is the range of the first 15 minutes after market open. Mark the high and low of that range — these become critical levels for the day.

Step 3: A+ Entries Using Confluence

This is where the magic happens. To get an A+ entry, you need multiple reasons stacked on top of each other:

ORB Break and Retest

Wait for price to break outside the NTZ and outside the ORB range. Then — and this is critical — don't chase the breakout candle. Wait for the price to come back and retest.

That retest, combined with a clean structure like a flag, gives you a high-probability entry with tight risk.

VWAP Rejections

VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price) is the only intraday level that smart money truly cares about. The rule is dead simple:

  • Above VWAP = Bullish. VWAP acts as support.
  • Below VWAP = Bearish. VWAP acts as resistance.

When you see price bounce off VWAP in the direction of the trend? That's confluence.

Level Flag Entries

This is the continuation pattern that pays me every single day. Find a key hourly level. If you see a flag forming above or below it (a tight consolidation), wait for the flag break.

Entry = flag break. Stop-loss = other side of the flag. Target = next hourly level.

This is the highest probability setup I've ever traded. The risk is small, the reward is massive, and you only need one or two of these a day.

Why This Simple Options Strategy Works

It works because it's rule-based. There's no guessing, no emotion, no FOMO. You have:

  1. Key levels to define your battlefield
  2. Time frames and 200 SMA to confirm direction
  3. ORB + VWAP + flags for precise entries

That's it. No magic indicators. No secret sauce. Just a repeatable framework that you execute the same way every morning.
