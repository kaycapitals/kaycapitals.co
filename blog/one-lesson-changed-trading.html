This ONE Lesson Changed My Trading Forever — And It'll Change Yours Too

By Somesh | Kay Capitals University

Has this ever happened to you? You wake up early. You plan your levels. You do your entire pre-market routine — journal, news, charts, everything. You know exactly where you want to enter. But the moment it's time to actually execute... you freeze.

Your brain throws some random thought at you: What if it doesn't work? What if I lose money? What if I'm wrong? And just like that, the move happens without you.

I've been there. I'm Somesh, I've been day trading for about eight years now, and early in my career this was my biggest problem. Not the strategy. Not the analysis. The execution. And today I'm going to share the best trading lesson I ever received.

You're Not Losing Because of Bad Plans

Let me be blunt: you're not losing because you don't know how to make a plan. You're losing because you don't know how to execute the plan. And execution is 80% psychology, 20% skill.

The Three Things You Must Know Before Every Trade

  1. Position size — How much money am I putting into this trade?
  2. Stop-loss — How much do I stand to lose?
  3. Profit target — How much do I stand to make?

If you don't know these three things, you will hesitate. Even if you force yourself to enter, your brain will panic at the first pullback.

The Story That Changed Everything

Early 2020. I woke up, did everything perfectly. Journaled my old trades, reviewed the news, drew my levels. By 8:30 AM Eastern, I had the cleanest plan I'd ever made.

But when the market opened, it was super bearish. And my plan was entirely bullish. Instead of adapting, I got married to my plan. I forced long setups on a bearish day. I lost way more money than I want to admit.

That was lesson number one: always be flexible.

But the bigger lesson came from a different day. I had the perfect setup. The market was literally handing me free money. And I hesitated. By the time I was ready to pull the trigger, the stop-loss was too wide. So I told myself, "The risk-reward doesn't make sense anymore." But the real reason? I was scared.

"What If It Worked?"

I called a friend — a trader I respected. He said one sentence:

"Bro, what if it worked?"

Then he said something even more powerful:

"If you know your stop-loss, there's literally nothing in the stock market that can hurt you."

That hit me like a truck. Because he was right. I knew my stop-loss. I knew exactly what I stood to lose. So why was I scared?

This is the best trading lesson I've ever received. Confidence comes from having a defined risk.

The Two Lessons That Will Transform Your Trading

Lesson 1: Always Be Flexible
If the market opens bearish and your plan says bullish — throw the bullish plan away. Don't marry your bias. Make a plan for both directions.

Lesson 2: If You Know Your Stop-Loss, You're Untouchable
Confidence doesn't come from prediction. It comes from preparation. When you know your position size, your stop-loss, and your target, there's no scenario that can break you.

As I tell my students at Kay Capitals University: Be careful when you're planning, but be bold when you're executing.

The Execution Framework

Before every trade, ask yourself:

  1. What's my position size?
  2. Where's my stop-loss?
  3. What's my profit target?

If you can answer all three, pull the trigger. If you can't, step away.

Stop Hesitating. Start Executing.

Most traders don't need a better strategy. They need a better relationship with risk. They need to stop asking "what if it fails?" and start asking "what if it works?"
