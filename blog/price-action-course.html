The Complete Price Action Trading Course: From Zero to Actually Making Money

By Somesh | Kay Capitals University

This is the price action trading course I wish existed when I started. No fluff, no theory that sounds good but doesn't work in real markets, no "just buy when the candle is green" nonsense. This is everything you need to know about trading — from basic terminology to the exact three-step framework I use to day trade profitably.

I'm Somesh, full-time day trader since 2018, founder of Kay Capitals University, and I've trained over a thousand students. I lost money for my first year and a half. Then I figured it out. Everything I figured out is in this course. If you're new to trading, also start with our stock market basics guide to build a solid foundation.

The Foundation: Basic Terminology You Actually Need

Stocks, Bulls, and Bears

A stock represents ownership in a company. Bulls are buyers — they believe prices will rise. Think of a bull striking upward with its horns. Bears are sellers — they believe prices will fall. Bears strike downward.

Bid, Ask, and Spread

The bid is the highest price someone will pay to buy. The ask is the lowest price you'll accept to sell. The spread is the difference between them. The narrower the spread, the easier it is to get filled — and the less money you lose on entry and exit.

Long, Short, and Scalping

Long = you think price is going up. You buy first, sell later. Short = you think price is going down. You sell first, buy back later. Scalping = catching tiny moves with large size. Quick in, quick out.

I'll be honest — I think scalping is often just a fancy word for "I'm too scared to hold." On my easy days, I'm catching multi-dollar moves. On choppy days? Sure, I'll scalp. But I let the market dictate my style, not my fear.

How to Read Candlesticks — For Real

Every candlestick has four data points: Open, High, Low, Close (OHLC).

What the Body Tells You

  • Long body = strong buying or selling pressure
  • Short body = weak pressure — nobody really cares
  • Marubozu candle = full body, no wicks at all. The most powerful candles you'll see.

What the Wicks Tell You

  • Long upper wick, short lower wick = sellers took over. Bearish signal.
  • Long lower wick, short upper wick = buyers took over. Bullish signal.

Key Candlestick Patterns

Bullish Harami: A large red candle followed by a small green candle that fits entirely within the red candle's body. Usually signals a reversal to the upside.

Bearish Engulfing: A green candle completely swallowed by a larger red candle. Signals potential downside.

Hammer: A candle with a tiny body and a long lower wick, appearing at support. Strong bullish signal.

Evening Star Doji: A three-candle pattern — bullish candle, tiny doji that gaps up, then a bearish candle. Signals potential reversal to the downside.

Important: Never trade candlestick patterns in isolation. They only matter at critical levels. A bullish hammer in the middle of nowhere means nothing. A bullish hammer at a major daily support zone? That's a trade. For a deeper dive into identifying these critical levels, check out our support and resistance guide.

Support and Resistance: Where the Money Lives

What Creates Support and Resistance?

Think supply and demand. Support = the floor. Where buyers step in. Price drops, hits support, bounces. Resistance = the ceiling. Where sellers step in. Price rises, hits resistance, rejects.

How to Find Levels

I use two time frames for levels:

  • Daily chart: Mark the major, critical levels with one color
  • 60-minute chart (with extended hours on): Mark intraday levels with another color

That's it. Don't draw levels on the 30-minute, 15-minute, 5-minute, 1-minute charts. You'll end up with so many lines you can't trade. Keep it simple. Less is more.

How to Trade Breakouts Without Getting Faked Out

Most breakouts fail. I'd say 65% of them don't work. The key to avoiding fakeouts: wait for consolidation before the breakout.

If price just rams into a level with no pause — that's usually a false breakout. But if price approaches a level, consolidates for one or two candles, and then breaks — that's a much higher probability setup. During consolidation, option premiums cool down. Your risk is tighter. Your stop loss is clear.

My preferred way to trade breakouts: the retest. Let it break. Let it come back and test the level from the other side. Enter on the retest with a tight stop. This is where the best risk-to-reward lives.

The Three-Step Day Trading Framework

This is the price action strategy that's made me millions:

Step 1: Identify the Trend

In the first 15-20 minutes, I watch for big range candles. Strong green candles with good volume = trending up. Strong red candles = trending down.

Step 2: Wait for a Pullback to Key EMAs

I use the 8 EMA and 20 EMA. On a trending day, when price pulls back to these moving averages — that's where I'm looking for entries. Options are cheapest at pullbacks. That's where I size up.

Step 3: Enter at Confluence

The ideal entry has multiple things lining up: the EMA, an intraday support level, and a bullish candle pattern (like a hammer). This confluence gives me confidence to pull the trigger with real size.

Risk Management: The Non-Negotiable

Before every trade, I need four things:

  1. Entry price — Where am I getting in?
  2. Stop loss — Where am I wrong?
  3. Profit target — Where am I taking money?
  4. Rationale — Why am I taking this trade?

If I can't answer all four, I don't trade.

Trailing Stop Loss Strategy

As the trade moves in my favor: after first big move, move stop to break even. After next consolidation breakout, move stop up to new support. Keep moving the stop higher with each consolidation break. This is how you catch trades of $100, $200, $500+ on options.

Daily Habits of a Profitable Day Trader

  • Sleep: Find your optimal amount and stick to it
  • Meditation: 15-20 minutes every morning. Non-negotiable
  • Cold showers: Best daily discipline hack I've ever used
  • Journaling: Every single day. Back test. Review your trades. Consistency builds the muscle
  • Discipline outside trading: If you're disciplined in your daily life, that discipline bleeds into your trading

The Bottom Line

Learn price action trading properly and you have a skill for life. Not some indicator that works for two months and then stops. An actual understanding of how price moves, why it moves, and how to profit from it. This foundation becomes even more powerful when applied to options trading strategies where timing and precision matter most.
