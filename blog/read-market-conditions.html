How to Read the Market: Go Easy When It's Hard, Go Hard When It's Easy

By Somesh | Kay Capitals University

This is literally the secret to making money in the stock market, and most traders never learn it: go easy when the market is hard, and go hard when the market is easy.

Most traders don't know how to recognize whether the market is hard or easy on any given day. They trade the same way regardless. Same size, same intensity, same number of trades. And that's exactly why they're inconsistent.

I'm Somesh, day trader and founder of Kay Capitals University, and I'm going to give you the exact framework I use to identify market conditions for day trading.

What Makes a Market Day "Hard" or "Easy"?

Hard market days = your system isn't performing the way it normally does.

Easy market days = your system is perfectly in sync with the market.

It's not about the market. It's about how well your strategy aligns with current conditions.

The 3-Step Framework to Recognize Hard Market Days

1. Your System Starts Playing Up

Your strategy normally works. Today, it's not delivering the usual results. The first trade didn't work. The second trade gave you a small profit but immediately reversed.

2. Trades Feel Forced

You're in a trade and something feels off. The volume isn't there. Every little pullback hurts more than the move pays.

3. You Start Questioning Your Strategy

After a couple of losing trades, you start thinking: "Does this even work?" You throw out your trend analysis and start trading random candle patterns.

What to Do When the Market Is Hard

Reduce your position size. Limit your number of trades. I use the 3-2-1 rule. The moment I identify it's a hard day, I count down — 3, 2, 1 — and I close my broker.

Hard market days are about survival, not maximizing gains.

The 3-Step Framework to Recognize Easy Market Days

1. Your System Is Performing Seamlessly

Trades align perfectly. Market gives you entries within the first 15-20 minutes.

2. Execution Feels Effortless

You take a trade and the moment you enter, it just goes. You hit your first profit target in minutes.

3. Your System Works Across the Board

You look at multiple charts — different tickers, different setups — and your system is working on all of them.

What to Do When the Market Is Easy

1. Increase Your Position Size — Use your early morning gains as a cushion.

2. Hold Your Trades Longer — Take profits at the first target. Move stop to break even. Let the runner ride.

3. Add to Winning Positions — When the market gives you another entry, why not add?

4. Let Your Runners Run — Hold through the structure. Only exit when structure breaks.

5. Take Calculated Risks — Easy market days happen maybe three or four times a month. When they show up, you can't be timid.

The Secret: Show Up Every Day

You can't predict when easy market days will happen. So you show up every single day. Start fresh, execute your system, and adapt based on what the market gives you.
