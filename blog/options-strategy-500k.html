The "Boring" Options Strategy That Made $500K in One Month — Full Breakdown

By Somesh | Kay Capitals University

My strategy is boring. Like, really boring. But despite that, it made me over half a million dollars in the month of October alone.

Now before you get excited — that's my personal result. It's not typical. It's not something you'll replicate by watching one YouTube video. But what I can share is the exact framework behind the Kay Capitals options strategy, the rules that make it work, and a full backtest so you can see it in action.

I'm Somesh, I've been day trading for eight-plus years. I've blown accounts, rebuilt them, tested everything you can imagine. And after all of that, I realized something that most traders never will: boring plus repeatable beats exciting every single time.

Why This Profitable Options Strategy Is "Boring"

It's boring because it's rule-based. It tells me when I'm allowed to trade and when I'm not, when to size up and when to size down, when to hold and when to cut, when to take partials and when to let runners breathe.

There's no guessing. No gut feelings. No FOMO chasing. And that's exactly why it works.

I only trade outside the No Trading Zone. I trade in the direction of the trend using time frames and 200 SMA. I enter using Opening Range Breakouts with clean structure. I size using bell curve positioning and let runners run on trend days.

That's the entire strategy.

The 90-Minute Rule

You do not need more than 90 minutes to day trade. The first 90 minutes after market open gives you the cleanest moves. After that, it gets choppy and slow — and that's where most traders give back everything they made.

My job isn't to trade all day. My job is to show up, execute A+ setups, and get out. That's how to make money trading options consistently — not by grinding for eight hours, but by being surgical for 90 minutes.

Step 1: Mark Your Levels

Every morning, before I even think about entries, I mark my levels on a 60-minute line chart with extended hours turned on. I draw at every bend — every place price clearly turned, paused, or rejected.

Critical mindset shift: Most people use levels to enter trades. I use levels to exit trades. They become my profit-taking zones.

Step 2: Define Your No Trading Zone

The NTZ is built using four levels: pre-market high, pre-market low, yesterday's high, yesterday's low.

If price is stuck inside this box, I'm not trading. Period. Inside the NTZ is where beginners get destroyed — false breakouts, trap wicks, choppy algorithm games.

Sitting on your hands is a position. Cash is a position.

Step 3: Confirm Direction

I use different time frames at different times of day:

  • First 30 min: 2-minute chart
  • 10:00 – 11:00: 5-minute chart
  • After 11:00: 10-minute chart

Then I apply the 200 SMA: above it = calls, below it = puts, chopping around it = no trade.

Step 4: Enter on A+ Setups Only

I wait for the ORB break and retest. I never chase breakouts — I wait for price to come back, form clean structure (usually a flag), and then enter on the continuation.

The confluence stack:

  • ORB break and retest ✓
  • Flag structure ✓
  • VWAP alignment ✓
  • 200 SMA direction ✓
  • Key level proximity ✓

When three or more of these align, that's an A+ setup. That's when I press the button.

Step 5: Bell Curve Sizing

  • First trade of the day: Small size. Always.
  • Market is clean, I'm in rhythm: Size up gradually.
  • I'm red or unsure: Stay small or stop trading entirely.
  • Never go max size on trade number one.

On clean trend days, I let runners run. That's how average days turn into $100K+ days. Not from one magical entry, but from the market trending clean and me not panicking out.

Full Backtest Walkthrough

I pulled up a random day — October 7th — using TradingView's replay feature with zero prior bias.

Pre-market prep: Marked hourly levels on a line chart. Identified pre-market high and low. Drew the NTZ box. Checked for Fed speakers.

Market opens: Waited for first 15 minutes. ORB forms. Price broke below the ORB and NTZ. Waited for the retest — didn't enter on the retest candle, waited for the NEXT candle (this is key).

Result: Massive move to the downside. Took profits at each level. Three clean entries, one framework, one random day. All trades profitable.

Why This Framework Compounds

This profitable options strategy isn't exciting. You're not taking 20 trades a day. You're not glued to screens for eight hours. But over time, it compounds because:

  • Losses stay small (defined stops, small first trades)
  • Winners get room to breathe (runners on trend days)
  • You're only taking the highest-probability setups
  • Your psychology stays intact because you're not overtrading
