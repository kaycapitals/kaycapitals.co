Day Trading Questions Answered: Your Biggest Trading Problems, Solved

By Somesh | Kay Capitals University

I get hundreds of trading questions every week on Instagram, Discord, and YouTube. Most of them come down to the same handful of problems — bad entries, holding losers, fear of losing, no strategy, and not knowing when to exit.

So I sat down and answered them all in one session. I'm Somesh, full-time day trader and founder of Kay Capitals University, and this is your day trading FAQ.

"How Do I Hold My Winners?"

This is the number one question I get. The root cause: you don't have a strategy that tells you when to exit.

If you're holding because someone else told you to buy, you have no exit plan. You don't know when to get out because you didn't know why you got in.

The fix: You need a strategy with clear rules. Entry. Stop loss. Profit target. If the trade is working — structure is intact — you hold. If structure breaks, you exit.

"How Do I Minimize Fakeouts?"

About 65% of breakouts fail. The only ones I trade are breakout-and-retest setups, where price breaks a level, pulls back to it, and I enter on the retest. Much better risk-to-reward.

Wait for consolidation before the breakout. If price just rams into a level with no pause — that's usually a false breakout.

"What Are Your Highest Probability Setups?"

A pullback entry on a trending day. In the first 15-20 minutes, I identify a strong trend. Then I wait for the stock to pull back to key EMAs — like the 8 EMA or VWAP. When it bounces, that's my entry.

Options are cheapest at pullbacks. That's when I size up.

"How Do I Move My Stop Loss Up While in Profit?"

  1. Enter the trade. Stop loss at your predetermined level.
  2. Price makes a big move. Bring stop loss to break even.
  3. Price consolidates, then breaks out again. Move stop loss up to the consolidation low.
  4. Repeat with every new consolidation breakout.

"Which Time Frame Should I Use for Support and Resistance?"

Daily chart — for major levels only. 60-minute chart (with extended hours on) — for intraday levels. That's it. Don't draw levels on every time frame.

"How Do I Deal With Fear of Losing?"

  1. Trade with money you can afford to lose. If every trade feels like life or death, your position size is too big.
  2. Build your back-testing statistics. When you've seen a setup work 60 out of 100 times, you stop fearing individual losses.

"What Daily Habits Make a Profitable Trader?"

  1. Sleep — Find your sweet spot
  2. Meditation — 15-20 minutes every morning
  3. Cold showers — The best discipline hack
  4. Back-testing/journaling — Every single day
  5. Discipline outside trading — Eat clean. Show up on time.

"How Do I Come Back After Red Days?"

Don't try to make the money back. Focus on one thing: break the losing chain.

Come in with tiny size. Make $20. Close your broker. Chain broken. The next day, your entire psychology resets.
