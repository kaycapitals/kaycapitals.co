The 3-Step A+ Options Trading Strategy That Changed Everything

By Somesh | Kay Capitals University

I've been trading for about eight years. In that time, I've backtested over 30,000 trades, tested dozens of indicators and time frames, and tried just about every strategy under the sun. And after all of that, the best options trading strategy I've ever found can be explained in three steps.

I call it the A+ strategy. And in this post, I'm going to break the entire system down so you can see exactly why it works — and how you can start using it.

This isn't flashy. It's not exciting. But this simple options strategy that works has produced days that most people wouldn't believe. And the beauty of it? You only need to trade the first 90 minutes of the day. For a more advanced approach, also check out our advanced options strategy that made $500K in one month.

The 90-Minute Rule That Changed My Life

The first 90 minutes after market open gives you 80% of the cleanest moves for the entire day. After that, the market gets slow, gets choppy, and that's where most traders hand back every dollar they made.

Trade the open. Execute your setups. Walk away. Less stress, less noise, more consistency. If you struggle with executing your plan consistently, our guide on fixing trading mental blocks will help you stay disciplined.

Step 1: Identify Your Key Levels

This is where 99% of traders mess up. Here's exactly what you do:

Go to a 60-minute chart. Turn on extended trading hours. Switch to a line chart. Draw levels at every bend you see on the line. Three or four levels above current price, three or four below. Switch back to candlesticks and fine-tune.

These hourly levels are the backbone of the entire options trading strategy. For more detailed guidance on identifying these critical price points, read our complete support and resistance guide.

Mark Your Pre-Market Highs and Lows

Before the bell rings, mark your pre-market high and low. If prices are stuck between the pre-market high and low — or between yesterday's high and low — you're in the No Trading Zone (NTZ). You do not trade inside that box. Period.

How to Actually Use Levels

Here's the mindset shift: most traders use levels to get INTO trades. I use levels to get OUT of trades. My levels become profit-taking zones.

Step 2: Confirm the Trend Direction

Time Frame Structure

  • First 30 minutes: 2-minute chart (fast moves, opening range breakout)
  • 10:00 AM – 11:00 AM: 5-minute chart (market slows down)
  • After 11:00 AM: 10-minute chart (clean momentum)

The 200 SMA: Your Truth Filter

  • Price above 200 SMA → Bullish bias (think calls)
  • Price below 200 SMA → Bearish bias (think puts)
  • Price chopping around it → Stay out

Opening Range Breakout (ORB)

The Opening Range Breakout is the range of the first 15 minutes after market open. Mark the high and low — these become critical levels for the day.

Step 3: A+ Entries Using Confluence

To get an A+ entry, you need multiple reasons stacked on top of each other:

ORB Break and Retest

Wait for price to break outside the NTZ and ORB range. Then — don't chase the breakout candle. Wait for the price to come back and retest. That retest, combined with a clean flag structure, gives you a high-probability entry with tight risk.

VWAP Rejections

VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price) is the only intraday level that smart money truly cares about. Above VWAP = Bullish. Below VWAP = Bearish. When price bounces off VWAP in the direction of the trend? That's confluence.

Level Flag Entries

Find a key hourly level. If a flag forms above or below it, wait for the flag break. Entry = flag break. Stop-loss = other side of the flag. Target = next hourly level.

This is the highest probability setup I've ever traded.

Why This Simple Options Strategy Works

It works because it's rule-based. No guessing, no emotion, no FOMO. You have levels to define your battlefield, time frames and 200 SMA to confirm direction, and ORB + VWAP + flags for precise entries.

Over the past 20 trading days, this exact strategy hit over a 93% win rate with clean risk-to-reward on every move.
