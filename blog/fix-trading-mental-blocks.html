How to Unf*ck Your Mind So Day Trading Actually Becomes Easy

By Somesh | Kay Capitals University

If you're trading options and your emotions take over the moment you see $100 in profit — or the second a pullback happens and you panic sell — then this is the trading mindset reset you've been looking for.

After 8 years of day trading, I've learned one thing that separates profitable traders from everyone else: most traders don't struggle with finding entries. They struggle with holding runners and controlling their emotions.

I'm Somesh, founder of Kay Capitals University, and I'm about to break down the exact four methods I use to hold my winners, stop panic selling, and make bigger money.

Why Small Accounts Need Big Moves

If you're trading with a $300 position size and you catch a 10% move, that's $30. After commissions and fees — you barely break even.

Small accounts need bigger moves. And big moves only come when you let your runners run.

Rule 1: Hide Your P&L

Stop looking at your profit and loss while you're trading.

Every time you glance at your P&L, you trigger emotions. On Interactive Brokers — the broker I use — I literally removed the P&L tab from my screen years ago. Because I don't know if I'm up or down, I'm not scared of losing something — which automatically makes me hold my winners longer.

Rule 2: The Break-Even Runner System

  1. Enter with at least two contracts (say, at $120 each)
  2. Price hits your first profit target — contracts are at $150
  3. Sell one contract at $150 (locked in $30 profit)
  4. Immediately set a break-even stop loss on your second contract at $120

Now your runner is at zero risk. One contract pays you. The second contract either breaks even or pays you more. There is no scenario where you lose on the trade.

Rule 3: Trail Your Stop Loss on the Stock — Not the Option

Most traders set their stop loss based on how their option premium looks. That's emotional. Instead, base your stop loss on the stock price structure. If the stock is in an uptrend — making higher highs and higher lows — every time a new higher low forms, you move your stop up.

On trending days, this is how I catch $1, $5, $10, $15, even $20 moves.

Rule 4: Remove the Bids and Asks From Your Option Chain

Even after hiding my P&L, my brain would still quickly calculate my profit from the bid-ask prices. So I removed them. I took the bid and ask prices completely off my option chain display.

When you stop seeing premiums jump around, you stop panicking. You stop micromanaging every candle. You start focusing on levels, price action, and structure — not emotional noise.

Why This Works

When you hide your P&L, set break-even stops, trail on structure, and remove bid-ask from your chain — you eliminate roughly 80% of the emotional mistakes most traders make.

You don't need more indicators. You don't need more setups. You need the discipline and conviction to hold your trades for the bigger move.
